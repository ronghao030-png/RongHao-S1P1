怦然 When Petals Fell

初恋 Blush of Spring

第一次遇到你，是一个无比普通的春天。我和你对上双眼，恰好樱花落在我们鼻尖。那是少年的第一次心动，第一次，也是永远。从此之后，每当说起春天，我都会形容，那是一个树上开满樱花的季节。
It was a perfectly ordinary spring. Until I met your eyes, and a single cherry blossom landed on the bridge of our noses. That moment—so fleeting, so foolishly tender— was my very first love. Since then, when I speak of spring, I speak of the season when the trees bloomed just for us.

鎏金岁月 Golden Reverie

那天，我骑着自行车载她穿过麦田。阳光瀑布一般倾泄在我们身上，金光灿烂。风吹乱了你的头发，我听见大大的世界里，只有你轻轻的歌唱。
That day, I rode my bike through the wheat fields with you on the backseat. The sun poured over us like a waterfall, casting everything in molten gold. The wind tangled your hair, and somewhere in the vastness of the world, I heard only the softness of your song.

遗憾 What Could Have Been

海非深 The Sea Wasn’t Deep

这是曾经我们去过的那片海，偶而有飞鸟掠过深蓝色的海面，这时我便想起了你。那是一个，海鸥都不再喧闹的傍晚。你蹦蹦跳跳地走在前面，而我踩着你的脚印前行。多好的一个夜晚，连风都安静。可为什么，为什么我没有珍惜。 “相恨不如潮有信，相思始觉海非深”
That was the sea we once knew. A few birds skimmed the surface of the blue, and in their silence, I remembered you. You skipped ahead, laughing. I followed your footprints in the sand, pretending I’d always be just a few steps behind. The wind was quiet. The night was kind. So why— why didn’t I hold on to it?

君记否 A Pressed Leaf

直到我又打开那封信，才发现夹在缝隙里的相思叶。那是你承诺要带我去的南方，是候鸟栖息的山岗。冬天那么漫长，我也再不会收到你的信。可是亲爱的，那个你种在我心里的，四季如春的地方，又让我如何忘记。
I opened the letter again today. A pressed leaf fell from between the folds— the kind that only grows in the South, where the birds used to rest. You promised we’d go there. But winter came and stayed, and your words stopped arriving. Still, the place you planted in my heart remains in bloom, and I don’t know how to forget the spring.

重逢 When We Met Again

半江红 Crimson Riverlight

很久之后，我在夕阳的余晖下认出了你的身影。你朝我走来，落日染红半江潮水。两盏回忆在这里相遇，我竟不知所措。你用手指抹过我的眼角，这是我们如水般澄澈的久别。
I saw you again— just as the sun dipped low and dyed the river red. You walked toward me, and suddenly the tide shimmered with memory. Two silhouettes. Two hearts that never quite forgot. You brushed a tear from the corner of my eye, and in that moment, I realized: this was the clearest water we'd ever shared.

火树银花 Blossoms of Light

“我们去看烟花好吗？” 那是我们重逢的第二天。在蓝调时分，我突然想起你说过的话。还好天色未晚，还好这次我没忘记，还好你还在我身边。 亲爱的，在这盛开的光影下，枯木逢春风，火树生银花。
"Let’s go see the fireworks." It was the second day after we found each other again. The sky was still a shade of blue that held its breath. Then I remembered what you once said. This time— I didn’t forget. This time— you were still by my side. And under the blooming light, even the withered trees dared to bloom again.
